## 1. Schema Therapy Overview

Schema Therapy is an integrative therapeutic method developed by **Jeffrey E. Young, Ph.D.**

**Key reference**: Young, JF, Klosko, JS, Weishaar ME: Sématerápia. VIKOTE, Budapest, 2010.

### Four Main Concepts
1. **Early Maladaptive Schemas** (Korai Maladaptív Sémák)
2. **Core Emotional Needs** (Alapvető érzelmi szükségletek)
3. **Schema Modes** (Séma módok)
4. **Maladaptive Coping Styles** (Maladaptív megküzdési stílusok)

### Theoretical Roots (Elméleti gyökerek)
- Psychoanalysis (pszichoanalízis) - childhood experience exploration
- Cognitive Therapy (kognitív terápia) - active, directive, structured
- Gestalt Therapy - imagery, dialogues between self-parts
- Object Relations Theory (tárgykapcsolat-elméletek)

### Schema Therapy vs. Focused Cognitive Therapy
- Greater emphasis on basic developmental tasks
- Greater emphasis on deeply rooted core schemas
- Greater emphasis on the therapeutic relationship
- Greater emphasis on affects (imagery, role-play, movement therapy, emotional expression)
- More focus on childhood roots and developmental processes
- Greater emphasis on lifelong coping strategies (avoidance and compensation)
- Symptoms seen as maladaptive coping strategies blocking developmental tasks

## 3. The 5 Schema Domains (Sématartományok)

### Domain 1: DISCONNECTION & REJECTION (Elutasítottság / Kapcsolat megszakítása és elutasítás)
**Frustrated need**: Connection/Attachment (Kötődési igény)
**Background**: Unstable family: abusive, cold, rejecting, isolated
**Description**: Cannot form secure attachments; desire for stability and security will not be fulfilled.

**Schemas in this domain:**
- Emotional Deprivation (Érzelmi Depriváció)
- Abandonment/Instability (Elhagyatottság)
- Mistrust/Abuse (Bizalmatlanság, Abúzus)
- Defectiveness/Shame (Fogyatékosság)
- Social Isolation/Alienation (Társas izoláltság)

### Domain 2: IMPAIRED AUTONOMY & PERFORMANCE (Károsodott autonómia és teljesítmény)
**Frustrated need**: Competence & Autonomy (Kompetencia- és autonómia-igény)
**Background**: Enmeshed family, undermining child's confidence, overprotective, not supporting competence outside family

**Schemas in this domain:**
- Failure to Achieve (Kudarcra ítéltség)
- Dependence/Incompetence (Dependencia)
- Vulnerability to Harm or Illness (Sérülékenység)
- Enmeshment/Undeveloped Self (Behálózottság/Összegabalyodottság)

### Domain 3: IMPAIRED LIMITS (Károsodott korlátok)
**Frustrated need**: Setting Limits (Korlátok szabása)

**Schemas in this domain:**
- Entitlement/Grandiosity (Feljogosítottság, Grandiozitás)
- Insufficient Self-Control/Self-Discipline (Elégtelen önkontroll)

### Domain 4: OTHER-DIRECTEDNESS (Más általi irányítottság)
**Frustrated need**: Expression of needs and emotions (Szükségletek és érzelmek kifejezése)
**Background**: Inhibition, suppression of impulses and needs

**Schemas in this domain:**
- Subjugation (Leigázottság/Behódolás)
- Self-Sacrifice (Önfeláldozás)
- Approval-Seeking/Recognition-Seeking (Elismerés hajszolása)

### Domain 5: OVERVIGILANCE & INHIBITION (Fokozott éberség, aggályosság és gátlás)
**Frustrated need**: Spontaneity & Self-initiative (Spontaneitás)

**Schemas in this domain:**
- Unrelenting Standards/Hypercriticalness (Könyörtelen mércék)
- Negativity/Pessimism (Negativitás/Pesszimizmus)
- Emotional Inhibition (Túlkontrollálás)
- Punitiveness (Büntető készenlét)

## 4. The 18 Early Maladaptive Schemas - Full Descriptions

### Schema Definition (Séma definíciója)
- Result of innate temperament traits AND everyday harmful childhood experiences with parents, siblings, and peers
- Capable of producing high-intensity, disruptive affects
- Have self-defeating consequences and/or cause significant harm to others
- Significantly impede fulfillment of basic needs (autonomy, connection, self-expression, acceptance, limits, spontaneity)
- Deeply embedded patterns, central to self-experience
- Mostly self-perpetuating
- Very difficult to change permanently with traditional techniques
- Activated by schema-relevant everyday events or biologically determined moods

### Formal Definition
An Early Maladaptive Schema is:
- A comprehensive, pervasive theme (Átfogó, pervazív téma)
- Concerning the relationship with oneself or others (Önmagával vagy másokkal való viszonyra vonatkozik)
- Develops during childhood, continues to evolve throughout life (Gyerekkor során alakul ki, az élet során tovább fejlődik)
- Significantly dysfunctional (Jelentős mértékben diszfunkcionális)

---

### 1. Abandonment / Instability (AB) - Elhagyatottság
The perceived instability or unreliability of those available for support and connection. Involves sensing that significant others will be emotionally unstable, unreliable, erratically present, or will abandon you.

### 2. Mistrust / Abuse (MA) - Bizalmatlanság, Abúzus
The expectation that others will hurt, abuse, humiliate, cheat, lie, manipulate, or take advantage. Usually involves perception that harm is intentional or results from extreme negligence, including feeling cheated relative to others.

### 3. Emotional Deprivation (ED) - Érzelmi Depriváció
Expectation that one's desire for a normal degree of emotional support will not be adequately met by others. Three forms:
- **(A) Deprivation of Nurturance** - absence of attention, affection, warmth, or companionship (támogatás)
- **(B) Deprivation of Empathy** - absence of understanding or mutual feeling-sharing (együttérzés)
- **(C) Deprivation of Protection** - absence of strength, direction, or guidance (védelem)

### 4. Defectiveness / Shame (DS) - Fogyatékosság
The feeling that one is defective, bad, unwanted, inferior, or invalid in important respects. May involve hypersensitivity to criticism, shame regarding perceived flaws (private or public), and feelings of unlovability.

### 5. Social Isolation / Alienation (SI) - Társas izoláltság
The feeling that one is isolated from the rest of the world, different from other people, and/or not part of any group or community.

### 6. Dependence / Incompetence (DI) - Dependencia
Belief that one is unable to handle one's everyday responsibilities in a competent manner, without considerable help from others. Often presents as helplessness regarding self-care, problem-solving, judgment, and decision-making.

### 7. Vulnerability to Harm or Illness (VH) - Sérülékenység
Exaggerated fear that imminent catastrophe will strike at any time and that one will be unable to prevent it. Fears focus on:
- **(A) Medical Catastrophes** - heart attacks, AIDS, etc.
- **(B) Emotional Catastrophes** - going crazy
- **(C) External Catastrophes** - plane crashes, criminal victimization

### 8. Enmeshment / Undeveloped Self (EM) - Behálózottság/Összegabalyodottság
Excessive emotional involvement and closeness with one or more significant others (often parents), at the expense of full individuation or normal social development. Often involves belief that enmeshed individuals cannot survive without constant support, feelings of being smothered, or insufficient individual identity.

### 9. Failure to Achieve (FA) - Kudarcra ítéltség
The belief that one has failed, will inevitably fail, or is fundamentally inadequate relative to one's peers, in areas of achievement (school, career, sports, etc.). Often involves beliefs about being stupid, inept, untalented, or less successful than others.

### 10. Entitlement / Grandiosity (ET) - Feljogosítottság, Grandiozitás
The belief that one is superior to other people; entitled to special rights and privileges; or not bound by the rules of reciprocity that guide normal social interaction. Often involves insistence on having whatever one wants regardless of realism or cost to others, or exaggerated focus on superiority to achieve power or control, sometimes with excessive competitiveness and domination.

### 11. Insufficient Self-Control / Self-Discipline (IS) - Elégtelen önkontroll
Pervasive difficulty or refusal to exercise sufficient self-control and frustration tolerance to achieve one's personal goals, or to restrain the excessive expression of one's emotions and impulses. In milder forms: exaggerated emphasis on discomfort-avoidance.

### 12. Subjugation (SB) - Leigázottság/Behódolás
Excessive surrendering of control to others because one feels coerced—usually to avoid anger, retaliation, or abandonment. Two forms:
- **(A) Subjugation of Needs** - suppression of preferences, decisions, and desires
- **(B) Subjugation of Emotions** - suppression of emotional expression, especially anger
Usually involves perception that one's desires are invalid, leading to excessive compliance, hypersensitivity to feeling trapped, and buildup of anger manifested in maladaptive behaviors.

### 13. Self-Sacrifice (SS) - Önfeláldozás
Excessive focus on voluntarily meeting the needs of others in daily situations, at the expense of one's own gratification. Common reasons: preventing others' pain, avoiding guilt, or maintaining connections with needy individuals. Often results from acute sensitivity to others' pain and may lead to resentment.

### 14. Approval-Seeking / Recognition-Seeking (AS) - Elismerés hajszolása
Excessive emphasis on gaining approval, recognition, or attention from other people, or fitting in, at the expense of developing a secure and true sense of self. Self-esteem depends primarily on others' reactions rather than natural inclinations, sometimes including overemphasis on status, appearance, or achievement for approval (not for power/control).

### 15. Negativity / Pessimism (NP) - Negativitás/Pesszimizmus
A pervasive, lifelong focus on the negative aspects of life (pain, death, loss, disappointment, conflict, guilt, resentment, unsolved problems, potential mistakes, betrayal, things that could go wrong, etc.) while minimizing the positive. Usually includes exaggerated expectations that things will go seriously wrong, with chronic worry, vigilance, complaining, or indecision.

### 16. Emotional Inhibition (EI) - Túlkontrollálás
The excessive inhibition of spontaneous action, feeling, or communication—usually to avoid disapproval by others, feelings of shame, or losing control of one's impulses. Most common areas:
- (a) inhibition of anger and aggression
- (b) inhibition of positive impulses (joy, affection, sexual excitement)
- (c) difficulty expressing vulnerability or communicating freely
- (d) excessive emphasis on rationality while disregarding emotions

### 17. Unrelenting Standards / Hypercriticalness (US) - Könyörtelen mércék
The underlying belief that one must strive to meet very high internalized standards of behavior and performance, usually to avoid criticism. Results in feelings of pressure and hypercriticalness toward oneself and others. Must involve significant impairment in pleasure, relaxation, health, self-esteem, or relationships. Typically presents as perfectionism, rigid rules and "shoulds," or preoccupation with time and efficiency.

### 18. Punitiveness (PU) - Büntető készenlét
The belief that people should be harshly punished for making mistakes. Involves the tendency to be angry, intolerant, punitive, and impatient with those who do not meet one's expectations or standards. Usually includes difficulty forgiving mistakes due to reluctance to consider extenuating circumstances or empathize with feelings.

## 5. Young Schema Questionnaire (YSQ) - Scoring & Evaluation

### Versions
- **YSQ-3 (3rd Edition)** - Current version, both Long Form and Short Form
- Long Form: more items reflecting subtle nuances, includes scoring key and grid
- Short Form: 5 items per schema (highest loading items), faster to administer, more pure factorially
- Reliability and factor structures are equivalent for both forms

### YSQ-2 (2nd Edition) Schema Abbreviations
| Code | Schema |
|------|--------|
| ed | Emotional Deprivation |
| ab | Abandonment |
| ma | Mistrust/Abuse |
| si | Social Isolation |
| ds | Defectiveness/Shame |
| su | Social Undesirability (discontinued in YSQ-3) |
| fa | Failure |
| di | Dependence/Incompetence |
| vh | Vulnerability to Harm & Illness |
| em | Enmeshment |
| sb | Subjugation |
| ss | Self Sacrifice |
| ei | Emotional Inhibition |
| us | Unrelenting Standards |
| et | Entitlement |
| is | Insufficient Self-Control/Self-Discipline |

### Scoring Methodology

#### Rating Scale
Items are rated on a 1-6 Likert scale:
- 1 = Completely untrue of me
- 2 = Mostly untrue of me
- 3 = Slightly more true than untrue
- 4 = Moderately true of me
- 5 = Mostly true of me
- 6 = Describes me perfectly

#### Raw Score Calculation
- Count items within each schema rated **5 or 6**
- Plot on Schema Grid for visual profile representation
- Grid converts raw scores to percentages (0-100%) for cross-schema comparison

#### Meaningful Thresholds
- **Long Form**: any score of **3 or more** on a schema is meaningful
- **Short Form**: any score of **2 or more** is meaningful

#### YSQ-3 Coverage
- Measures all **18** currently recognized Early Maladaptive Schemas
- YSQ-2 Long Form measured only **15** of 18 (excluded: Approval-Seeking, Negativity/Pessimism, Punitiveness)
- YSQ-2 Short Form also omitted Social Undesirability (discontinued)

## 6. Maladaptive Coping Styles (Megküzdési stílusok)

Three main categories (analogous to fight/flight/freeze):

### 6.1 Overcompensation ("Fight" - Túlkompenzáció)

| # | Coping Response | Description |
|---|----------------|-------------|
| 1 | Aggression, Hostility | Counterattacks through defying, abusing, blaming, attacking, or criticizing others |
| 2 | Dominance, Excessive Self-assertion | Controls others through direct means to accomplish goals |
| 3 | Recognition-seeking, Status-seeking | Overcompensates through impressing, high achievement, status, attention-seeking |
| 4 | Manipulation, Exploitation | Meets own needs through covert manipulation, seduction, dishonesty, or conning |
| 5 | Passive-aggressiveness, Rebellion | Appears compliant while punishing others covertly (procrastination, pouting, backstabbing, lateness, non-performance) |
| 6 | Excessive Orderliness, Obsessionality | Maintains strict order, tight self-control, high predictability through order, planning, routine, ritual, or undue caution |

### 6.2 Surrender ("Freeze" - Séma fenntartása/Bemerevedés)

| # | Coping Response | Description |
|---|----------------|-------------|
| 7 | Compliance, Dependence | Relies on others, gives in, seeks affiliation, passive, dependent, submissive, clinging, avoids conflict, people-pleasing |

### 6.3 Avoidance ("Flight" - Séma elkerülés)

| # | Coping Response | Description |
|---|----------------|-------------|
| 8 | Social withdrawal, Excessive autonomy | Social isolation, disconnection, withdrawal; exaggerated independence; retreats through TV, reading, solitary activities |
| 9 | Compulsive Stimulation-seeking | Excitement/distraction through compulsive shopping, sex, gambling, risk-taking, physical activity, novelty |
| 10 | Addictive Self-Soothing | Avoids through body-based addictions: alcohol, drugs, overeating, excessive masturbation |
| 11 | Psychological Withdrawal | Dissociation, numbness, denial, fantasy, or other internal forms of psychological escape |

### Additional Avoidance Forms (from Hungarian sources)
- Anger suppression (Harag elnyomása)
- Psychosomatic symptoms (Pszichoszomatikus tünetek)
- Avoiding social company (Társaság kerülése)
- Denial of memories (Emlékek tagadása)
- Self-soothing through eating, shopping (Önlenyugtatás)
- Sleep, lethargy-based avoidance (Alvás, energiátlanság általi elkerülés)

## 7. Schema Modes (Séma módok)

Schema modes are the currently active schemas, coping responses, or healthy behaviors - "moment-to-moment emotional states and coping responses" triggered by emotionally sensitive situations. They function as dissociated states along a spectrum from mild (normal mood shifts) to severe (DID).

### Four Mode Categories

#### Child Modes (Gyermek módok)
- **Vulnerable/Hurt Child** (Sérült) - feels frightened, sad, helpless
- **Angry Child** (Dühös) - enraged at unmet core needs
- **Impulsive Child** (Impulzív) - acts on desires without limits
- **Happy Child** (Boldog) - core needs currently met

#### Maladaptive Parent Modes (Szülői módok)
- **Punitive Parent** (Büntető Szülő) - internalized punishing parent voice
- **Demanding Parent** (Követelődző Szülő) - internalized demanding/critical parent voice

#### Maladaptive Coping Modes
- Correspond to the three coping styles (overcompensation, avoidance, surrender)

#### Healthy Adult Mode (Egészséges Felnőtt)
- The goal of schema therapy is to strengthen this mode
- Learns to navigate, negotiate with, nurture, or neutralize other modes

## 8. Schema Therapy Assessment Process (Felmérési folyamat)

### Phase 1: Assessment & Psychoeducation (Felmérés, pszichoedukáció)
1. Determine if patient is suitable for schema therapy
2. Identify main complaints
3. Establish therapeutic goals
4. Identify life-spanning patterns (Életen átívelő minták azonosítása)
   - Life history review
   - Connect present problems to recognizable life patterns
   - Identify emotions in problems and patterns

### Phase 2: Schema Assessment (Sémák felmérése)
1. **Patient fills out Schema Questionnaire** (Sémakérdőív kitöltetése)
2. Explain schema functioning to patient
3. Provide written descriptions of schema characteristics
4. **Evaluate the schema questionnaire** (Sémakérdőív kiértékelése)
5. Give brief description of patient's characteristic schemas, discuss them

### Phase 3: Assess Maladaptive Coping (Maladaptív megküzdési stratégiák felmérése)
- Use Schema Compensation Questionnaire
- Use Schema Avoidance Questionnaire
- Use any coping questionnaire
- Observe coping during and outside therapy sessions

### Phase 4: Explore Childhood Origins (Sémák gyermekkori eredetének feltárása)
1. Discuss childhood memories connected to schemas
2. Connect: Present problems <-> Schema <-> Childhood origin
3. Administer Young Parenting Inventory (Szülői kérdőív)
4. Explore connection between parental behavior and specific schemas
5. Clarify discrepancies between Schema Questionnaire and Parenting Questionnaire results

### Phase 5: Experiential Techniques in Assessment (Élményalapú technikák)
- Recall disturbing childhood images with mother, father, siblings, significant others
- Develop dialogues
- Ask patient what they needed in that scene
- Explore connection between childhood-image-activated emotions and present life

### Phase 6: Therapeutic Relationship in Assessment
- Monitor schema activation signs during sessions
- Draw patient's attention to these signs
- Collaboratively identify which schema activated and why
- Connect in-therapy schema-activating events to events outside therapy

### Phase 7: Schema Case Conceptualization (Séma esetkonceptualizáció)
1. Therapist fills in the case conceptualization chart
2. Present conceptualization summary to patient
3. Patient provides feedback, refine conceptualization
4. Agree on which life-spanning problem to work on first in the change phase

## 10. Schema-Focused Personality Diagnosis (Séma-Fókuszált Személyiség Diagnózis)

Four dimensions:
1. **Emotional Temperament** (Érzelmi temperamentum)
2. **Core Schemas** (Alap-sémák)
3. **Coping Style** (Megbirkózási stílus)
4. **Global Level of Dysfunction** (Diszfunkció globális szintje)

### Assessment Instruments
- Temperament and Character Inventory (TCI)
- Young Schema Questionnaire (YSQ)
- Young Parenting Inventory
- Young-Rygh Avoidance Inventory
- Young Compensation Inventory

### Signs of Long-lasting Life Problems (indicating schema therapy)
- Chronic and long-lasting problems
- Axis I disorder not responding to therapy
- Chronic relapse of Axis I disorder
- Problems not circumscribed yet pervasive
- Long-term relationship problems
- Very avoidant behavior
- Rigid thinking and behavioral patterns

## 11. Schema Therapy Change Phase Techniques

### Cognitive Techniques (Kognitív technikák)
- Examine validity of each schema across all life stages
- Collect evidence supporting AND contradicting schema
- Challenge schema-supporting evidence
- Apply reappraisal techniques
- Create dialogue between Healthy Adult side and Schema side
- Schema cue cards (Séma segédkártyák) for self-help
- Schema diary in later therapy stages

### Experiential Techniques (Élményalapú technikák)
- Imagery, dialogues
- Patient expresses anger and validates rights
- Grieving losses
- Confronting traumas and overcoming them
- Letter writing (usually not sent)

### Therapeutic Relationship (Terápiás kapcsolat)
- **Limited Reparenting** (Korlátozott szülői újragondoskodás): therapist acts as counterweight to original harmful parenting
- Therapist communicates that basic needs are valid and can be met
- Therapist adapts style to patient's schemas
- Patient internalizes therapist's healthy attitude
- Corrective emotional experience within therapeutic framework

### Behavioral Pattern Breaking (Viselkedéses minták megtörése)
- Identify self-defeating behavioral cycles
- Patient keeps diary of distressing events
- Therapist introduces new behaviors that break maladaptive cycles
- Practice through imagery, role-play, then outside therapy
- Homework assignments; use imagery to overcome psychological obstacles

## 12. Schema Self-Perpetuation Model

The schema creates a self-reinforcing cycle through two pathways:

**Cognitive & Perceptual Pathway** (Kognitív és érzékelési tünetek):
- Attention (Figyelem) -> Perception & Meaning Attribution (Percepció és Jelentés tulajdonítás) -> Confirmation (Igazolás) -> back to Schema

**Behavioral & Social Pathway** (Magatartás és társas tünetek):
- Action (Cselekvés) -> Response (Válasz) -> Confirmation (Igazolás) -> back to Schema

### Example: Mistrust/Abuse Schema
- Schema: "People are hostile, the world is dangerous, everyone is against me"
- Cognitive: Perceives hostility -> "People are angry at me"
- Behavioral: Aggressive style -> Gets attacked
- Both pathways confirm the schema
